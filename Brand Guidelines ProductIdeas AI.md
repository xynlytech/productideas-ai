# **Product Ideas AI Brand Guideline**

## **1\. Brand Overview**

**Product Ideas AI** is a demand intelligence platform that discovers product opportunities from search behavior, trend shifts, and unmet demand signals. The brand should feel intelligent, premium, sharp, and futuristic — a tool for serious founders, product teams, and operators.

This brand guideline is designed to align Product Ideas AI with the broader **Xynly** ecosystem: modern, minimal, digital-first, and highly scalable.

---

## **2\. Brand Essence**

### **Brand Purpose**

Help people discover what the world is actively searching for, before the market becomes obvious.

### **Brand Promise**

Turn search signals into actionable product ideas.

### **Brand Positioning**

Not a keyword tool. Not a random idea generator. Product Ideas AI is a **demand discovery engine**.

### **Core Differentiator**

The product does not just show trends. It explains opportunity:

* What people want  
* Why current solutions are insufficient  
* Where the market gap exists  
* What product could win

---

## **3\. Brand Personality**

### **Primary Traits**

* Intelligent  
* Futuristic  
* Precise  
* Insightful  
* Premium  
* Minimal  
* Data-driven

### **Secondary Traits**

* Bold  
* Fast-moving  
* Experimental  
* Foundational  
* Clear

### **Brand Feel**

The brand should feel like a mix of:

* A serious SaaS research tool  
* A sharp product analytics platform  
* A future-facing startup intelligence engine

It should never feel playful, childish, cluttered, or generic.

---

## **4\. Brand Voice**

### **Voice Principles**

* Clear over clever  
* Confident over noisy  
* Specific over vague  
* Insightful over promotional  
* Crisp over verbose

### **Tone by Context**

**Marketing pages:** energetic, aspirational, founder-focused

**Product UI:** concise, functional, data-first

**Error states:** calm, direct, helpful

**Emails / notifications:** professional, minimal, actionable

### **Example Voice**

Good:

* “Discover product opportunities backed by search demand.”  
* “Track rising demand before competitors do.”  
* “See why this idea matters.”

Avoid:

* “Unlock viral genius ideas instantly\!\!\!”  
* “The world’s craziest idea machine”  
* “Super fun AI for everyone”

## **6\. Logo System**

### **6.1 Logo Concept**

The logo is an abstract **P** with an upward motion / growth cue. It should symbolize:

* Product discovery  
* Search signal  
* Growth opportunity  
* Forward movement

### **6.2 Logo Style**

* Minimal  
* Geometric  
* Modern  
* Scalable  
* Favicon-ready  
* Works on dark and light backgrounds

### **6.3 Logo Usage Rules**

**Do:**

* Use the icon alone for app favicon, avatar, and small UI placements  
* Use the icon \+ wordmark together for hero sections and official branding  
* Maintain generous clear space around the mark

**Do not:**

* Stretch or skew the logo  
* Change icon proportions  
* Add outlines, drop shadows, or effects that reduce clarity  
* Place the logo on busy backgrounds  
* Recolor arbitrarily outside the approved palette

### **6.4 Clear Space**

Use padding around the logo equal to at least the width of the inner counter or the height of the icon stem, whichever is greater.

### **6.5 Minimum Size**

* App icon / favicon: simplified icon only  
* UI sidebar logo: icon at small size with legible wordmark  
* Hero logo: full size only, never compressed

## **7\. Color System**

The palette should match the Xynly-style visual language: dark-first, electric, premium, and futuristic.

### **7.1 Primary Palette**

* **Midnight Navy** — \#070B14  
* **Deep Blue Surface** — \#0D1422  
* **Slate Panel** — \#121A2A  
* **Electric Blue** — \#3B82F6  
* **Cyan Glow** — \#38BDF8  
* **Indigo Violet** — \#7C3AED  
* **Soft Purple** — \#A855F7  
* **White** — \#FFFFFF  
* **Muted Text** — \#94A3B8

### **7.2 Gradient System**

Primary brand gradient:

* Blue → Cyan → Violet

Secondary gradient:

* Indigo → Purple

Use gradients sparingly, mostly in:

* Logo  
* Highlights  
* Hero accents  
* Trend indicators  
* CTA emphasis

### **7.3 Color Roles**

**Backgrounds:** dark navy and slate tones

**Primary actions:** electric blue

**Highlights and emphasis:** cyan and violet gradient

**Success states:** green, used sparingly

**Warning states:** amber, used sparingly

### **7.4 Color Rules**

* Do not use saturated red as a primary brand color  
* Do not introduce random bright accent colors  
* Maintain high contrast for accessibility  
* Dark mode should be the default visual mode

---

## **8\. Typography**

### **8.1 Typeface Direction**

The typography should feel modern, technical, and clean.

### **8.2 Recommended Typeface Stack**

**Primary UI / Headings:**

* Space Grotesk, Inter, or Sora

**Body / Supporting Text:**

* Inter, IBM Plex Sans, or similar clean sans-serif

**Data / Metrics / Labels:**

* IBM Plex Mono or JetBrains Mono

### **8.3 Typography Rules**

* Headlines: sharp, confident, moderately bold  
* Body text: highly readable, neutral  
* Numbers and metrics: monospaced or semi-mono for clarity  
* Avoid decorative or overly rounded fonts

### **8.4 Hierarchy**

* H1: strong hero statements  
* H2: section headings  
* H3: card titles / feature labels  
* Body: concise explanations  
* Caption: metadata, timestamps, tags, source labels

---

## **9\. Iconography**

### **Icon Style**

* Simple line-based or solid geometric icons  
* Rounded corners where appropriate  
* Consistent stroke width  
* Minimal detail  
* Friendly but technical

### **Preferred Icon Themes**

* Search  
* Trend  
* Signal  
* Filter  
* Insight  
* Growth  
* Cluster  
* Save  
* Export

### **Avoid**

* Cartoon icons  
* Overly detailed illustrations  
* Filled emoji-style symbols  
* Mismatched icon styles across the interface

---

## **10\. Illustration Style**

If illustrations are used, they should be abstract and data-inspired.

### **Style Direction**

* Soft gradients  
* Floating signals  
* Abstract charts  
* Connected nodes  
* Orbital motion  
* Minimal depth

### **Avoid**

* Mascots  
* Playful doodles  
* Literal lightbulbs everywhere  
* Overly busy scenes

---

## **11\. UI Design Language**

### **11.1 Overall Style**

The product UI should be a **premium dark dashboard** with a data-first layout.

### **11.2 Surface System**

* Deep background  
* Lighter cards and panels  
* Thin borders with low-opacity white or blue-gray  
* Gentle glow accents on active elements

### **11.3 Shape Language**

* Rounded corners: 12px–16px  
* Smooth pill buttons for filters and tags  
* Compact cards with strong hierarchy

### **11.4 Layout Principles**

* High density but not cluttered  
* Clear scanning paths  
* Strong separation between sections  
* Search and filters should remain easy to find

### **11.5 Motion**

* Subtle hover elevation  
* Soft fades and slides  
* Slow ambient gradients  
* Smooth transitions, no bouncy gimmicks

---

## **12\. Data Visualization Style**

### **Charts**

* Clean line charts  
* Soft area fills  
* Minimal axis clutter  
* Subtle gridlines  
* Clear emphasis on trend direction

### **Scoring UI**

Opportunity score should be visually prominent and consistent.

Use:

* Badge  
* Ring indicator  
* Score bar  
* Compact numeric display

### **Trend Indicators**

* Upward arrows  
* Percentage growth values  
* Sparklines  
* Momentum labels such as “Rising,” “Accelerating,” or “Stable”

### **Rules**

* Do not overuse color  
* Avoid rainbow charts unless necessary  
* Keep chart labeling minimal and legible

---

## **13\. Product Messaging**

### **Core Message**

**Find product ideas people are already searching for.**

### **Supporting Messages**

* Discover unmet demand before the market is obvious  
* Turn search signals into product opportunities  
* Validate ideas with evidence, not intuition  
* See what people want and what the market still lacks

### **CTA Language**

Use action-oriented and insight-oriented CTAs such as:

* Explore Ideas  
* View Opportunity  
* Track Trend  
* Save Idea  
* Analyze Signal  
* Compare Demand

Avoid vague CTAs like:

* Learn More  
* Click Here  
* Start Now

unless they are used in a very specific context.

## **14\. Brand Photography and Imagery**

If photography is used in marketing pages:

* Use founders, builders, research, workspaces, dashboards, and product planning scenes  
* Keep images modern and realistic  
* Use dark or neutral palettes  
* Avoid stock-photo exaggeration

The ideal imagery should feel like:

* Product strategy  
* Search intelligence  
* Global market discovery  
* Founder execution

---

## **15\. Accessibility Principles**

### **Required Standards**

* Maintain readable contrast in dark mode  
* Ensure keyboard navigability  
* Use clear focus states  
* Do not rely on color alone to communicate state  
* Use descriptive labels for all controls

### **UI Accessibility Rules**

* Text never below readable sizes  
* Metadata remains legible on all screens  
* Interactive elements should have visible hover and focus states

---

## **16\. Brand Do’s and Don’ts**

### **Do**

* Keep the interface intelligent and clean  
* Use the logo consistently  
* Use the brand gradient as an accent, not a flood  
* Keep copy concise and useful  
* Preserve dark-first visual identity

### **Don’t**

* Use random neon colors  
* Mix too many illustration styles  
* Make the UI look like a toy  
* Overload with marketing hype  
* Change logo proportions or brand colors casually
