# **ProductIdeas AI — Pixel-Perfect UI/UX Prompt**

Design a **high-fidelity, production-ready SaaS web application UI** for a product called **ProductIdeas AI**.

This is a premium **demand intelligence platform** that helps founders discover product opportunities from search demand, trend acceleration, and unmet market needs. The interface must feel like a **serious, category-defining intelligence product**: sharp, modern, premium, data-rich, and visually memorable.

The UI should feel like a blend of **Linear, Stripe, Vercel, and Notion**, but with more **analytical depth, stronger visual hierarchy, and more energetic lighting**. It must not look flat, generic, or template-like.

---

## **1\) Overall Visual Direction**

Create a **dark-first SaaS interface** with deep layering, strong contrast, and controlled glow.

The aesthetic should be:

* premium  
* futuristic  
* minimal but not empty  
* information-dense but highly readable  
* elegant, confident, and founder-grade

The design should feel like a tool used by:

* startup founders  
* product managers  
* ecommerce operators  
* investors  
* growth teams

The UI must visually communicate:  
 **discovery, signal, opportunity, and momentum.**

---

## **2\) Core Design Principles**

Follow these principles strictly:

### **A. Depth over flatness**

Use multiple surface layers so the interface never feels like one flat dark panel. Build clear separation between:

* app background  
* content surfaces  
* cards  
* elevated states  
* active states

### **B. Contrast over monotony**

Avoid using too many similar dark grays. Each layer must be visually distinct.

### **C. Energy over dullness**

Use subtle gradients, glow, and lighting to create life in the interface. Not flashy. Controlled and premium.

### **D. Scanability over decoration**

The UI must be easy to scan quickly. Users should immediately identify:

* opportunity score  
* trend direction  
* category  
* region  
* confidence  
* problem statement

### **E. Decision-first design**

Every screen should help the user decide:

* is this opportunity strong?  
* is this worth saving?  
* is this worth building?

---

## **3\) Color System**

Use a dark and electric palette with clear layering.

### **Base Layers**

* Background: **\#05070D**  
* Surface 1: **\#0A0F1A**  
* Surface 2: **\#0F1626**  
* Surface 3 / Elevated: **\#131C31**  
* Border: **rgba(255,255,255,0.08)**

### **Accent Colors**

* Primary Blue: **\#3B82F6**  
* Cyan Glow: **\#38BDF8**  
* Indigo: **\#6366F1**  
* Violet Accent: **\#7C3AED**

### **Text Colors**

* Primary text: **\#FFFFFF**  
* Secondary text: **\#A1AABF**  
* Muted text: **\#6F778A**

### **Status Colors**

* Success: **\#22C55E**  
* Warning: **\#F59E0B**  
* Error: **\#EF4444**

### **Gradient Usage**

Use gradients **sparingly and intentionally** for:

* primary buttons  
* active score badges  
* hero accents  
* selected states  
* top opportunities

Preferred gradient:  
 **linear-gradient(135deg, \#3B82F6, \#38BDF8, \#6366F1)**

Do not overuse gradients. They should act as highlights, not background noise.

---

## **4\) Lighting and Glow System**

This is critical to avoid a dull-looking interface.

Use:

* soft radial glow behind hero content  
* subtle blue glow on primary CTA  
* faint glow around active cards  
* gentle highlight on selected score badges  
* smooth lighting transition on hover

Use shadows carefully:

* soft, large shadows for elevated cards  
* minimal shadow for base cards  
* stronger glow only for focused or active elements

Example feel:

* premium  
* intelligent  
* luminous  
* not neon club-style

---

## **5\) Typography**

Use a modern geometric hierarchy.

### **Recommended Font Direction**

* Headings: **Space Grotesk**  
* Body: **Inter**  
* Metrics/Data: **IBM Plex Mono**

### **Type Scale**

* H1: 56–64px, bold, tight line-height  
* H2: 32–40px, semibold  
* H3: 22–26px, semibold  
* Body: 15–16px, regular  
* Small metadata: 12–13px  
* Numeric labels: monospaced or semi-mono

### **Typography Rules**

* Headlines must feel sharp and authoritative  
* Body text must be clean and easy to read  
* Metrics must visually stand apart from description text  
* Avoid decorative or overly rounded fonts

---

## **6\) Layout and Grid**

### **Desktop Layout**

* 12-column grid  
* Max content width: **1200–1320px**  
* Outer page padding: **32px**  
* Section spacing: **64px–96px**  
* Card padding: **20px–24px**  
* Internal gap rhythm: **8, 12, 16, 24, 32**

### **Layout Feel**

* spacious but efficient  
* structured  
* not too loose  
* not cramped

The screen should feel like a premium analytics dashboard with strong editorial hierarchy.

---

## **7\) Core Components**

Design the following reusable components:

### **Sidebar**

* dark vertical sidebar  
* logo at top  
* navigation items with icons  
* active state with tinted background and left accent bar  
* compact, elegant, and highly legible

### **Top Bar**

* large search input  
* filter chips  
* optional quick actions on the right  
* sticky, polished, minimal

### **Idea Card**

Must include:

* title  
* opportunity score badge  
* short problem description  
* trend indicator  
* category tag  
* region tag  
* confidence label  
* save icon

Idea cards should be compact but information-rich. They must feel premium and clickable.

### **Score Badge**

* rounded pill  
* bold number  
* distinct color based on performance  
* must stand out as the visual anchor

### **Filter Chips**

* pill-shaped  
* minimal border  
* active state filled  
* hover state slightly elevated

### **Trend Indicator**

* arrow \+ percentage  
* optional small sparkline  
* color-coded by direction

### **Buttons**

* primary button: gradient \+ glow  
* secondary button: surface tone  
* ghost button: minimal, text-only or lightly bordered

---

## **8\) Page-by-Page UI Direction**

---

### **8.1 Landing Page**

Design a conversion-focused SaaS landing page.

#### **Hero Section**

* large headline  
* concise subheadline  
* primary CTA: **Explore Ideas**  
* secondary CTA: **View Demo**  
* subtle abstract background glow  
* dashboard preview mockup below the fold or alongside the hero

#### **Hero Content**

Headline:  
 **Find product ideas people are already searching for**

Subheadline:  
 **Turn global search behavior into validated product opportunities.**

#### **Below Hero**

Include:

* social proof strip  
* feature highlights  
* how it works section  
* sample opportunity cards  
* final CTA section

The landing page must feel premium and high trust, not generic or overly salesy.

---

### **8.2 Dashboard Page**

Design the main product dashboard.

#### **Layout**

* left sidebar  
* top search/filter bar  
* main content feed

#### **Main Content**

Show a ranked stream of opportunity cards. Cards should be displayed in a grid or dense vertical list depending on the emphasis on scanning.

Each card should show:

* problem-led title  
* score badge in top-right  
* one-line summary  
* trend growth indicator  
* tags  
* confidence level  
* save action

The dashboard should feel like a **live intelligence cockpit**.

---

### **8.3 Idea Detail Page**

Design a detailed opportunity page with two-column layout.

#### **Left Column**

* problem statement  
* explanation of why it matters  
* suggested product framing  
* trend chart  
* keyword cluster tags  
* related signal summary

#### **Right Column**

* opportunity score module  
* score breakdown  
* demand growth  
* pain intensity  
* competition  
* confidence  
* region  
* category  
* save button  
* export button

The page should feel analytical, structured, and decision-oriented.

---

### **8.4 Saved Ideas Page**

Design a personal workspace for saved opportunities.

Include:

* header  
* tabs: All, High Score, Recent  
* saved idea cards  
* note preview  
* date saved  
* quick edit and remove actions

Add a polished empty state:  
 **No saved ideas yet. Start exploring opportunities.**

---

### **8.5 Alerts Page**

Design an alerts control panel.

Include:

* active alerts list  
* create alert form  
* triggered opportunities section  
* toggles  
* thresholds  
* cadence selectors

The page should feel like a monitoring system.

---

### **8.6 Settings Page**

Design a minimal settings page with:

* profile  
* plan  
* notifications  
* data preferences  
* API access placeholder

Keep it clean and functional.

---

## **9\) Interaction Design**

Add subtle but premium interactions:

* hover \= slight lift \+ soft glow  
* active navigation item \= tinted surface \+ accent bar  
* primary CTA \= gradient with stronger shadow  
* card selection \= stronger border \+ glow  
* filter chips \= smooth state change  
* search input focus \= subtle accent border

Motion should be:

* smooth  
* restrained  
* polished  
* never flashy

---

## **10\) Content Hierarchy Rules**

The visual hierarchy must always prioritize:

1. opportunity score  
2. problem title  
3. trend direction  
4. supporting description  
5. metadata

Never let tags or metadata overpower the actual insight.

---

## **11\) Empty States and Microcopy**

Empty states should feel premium and encouraging, not generic.

Use crisp product language such as:

* “No saved ideas yet.”  
* “Start exploring opportunities.”  
* “No alerts match your current filters.”  
* “No ideas found for this query.”

Avoid playful or childish microcopy.

---

## **12\) Avoid These Mistakes**

Do not create:

* flat dark panels with no depth  
* washed-out gray cards  
* generic AI gradients everywhere  
* noisy colorful UI  
* too much whitespace with no content density  
* inconsistent card styles  
* weak typography hierarchy  
* low-contrast text  
* overdesigned “Dribbble-only” visuals

The result must look real, shippable, and expensive.

---

## **13\) Final Output Goal**

The final interface should feel like:

* a premium SaaS intelligence platform  
* a founder-grade product research tool  
* a polished and trustworthy data product  
* a high-value subscription platform with strong visual identity

It should communicate one idea clearly:

**The world is already searching. ProductIdeas AI shows what to build next.**
